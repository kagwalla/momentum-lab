# Auditor Report (Mode B) — Aniworld Veterinary Clinic

## Assets Reviewed

- Website: https://aniworldvetclinic.co.ke/ (WordPress/Elementor, built via
  Biznapages)
- Linked social: Facebook, Twitter/X, Instagram — links present on site,
  content not reviewed in this pass (would require live fetch of each
  platform)
- Two locations found: Kondele (Kisumu–Kakamega Rd, opposite Kondele
  Police Station) and Milimani (Owuor Otiende Rd, near Shady Gardens
  Academy)

## Gap Analysis

**1. Anxiety & Trust** (verified pain point)
- Present, partially. Site states KVB accreditation and "Quality Care"
  messaging. No fear-free/low-stress-handling language, no photos/video of
  the actual clinic interior or staff interacting with animals beyond one
  generic WhatsApp-sourced image. Trust signals are institutional
  (accreditation) rather than emotional (what a visit actually feels like).
- Gap severity: **Moderate** — the accreditation trust signal is real and
  usable, but nothing addresses the specific fear-of-clinical-environment
  angle Explorator identified.

**2. Friction to Book** (verified pain point)
- Present but weak. A "Book Appointment" button exists and links to a
  contact page — but this appears to be a contact form/phone number, not
  real-time booking. Three phone numbers are listed inconsistently across
  the page (formatting varies: "+254 572029971", "+254 57 20 29971",
  "+254 708 192 291"), which is a small but real friction/trust signal in
  itself.
- Gap severity: **High** — booking friction is the exact verified pain, and
  the current flow (call one of three inconsistently formatted numbers,
  hope someone answers) doesn't resolve it.

**3. Opaque Pricing** (verified pain point)
- Fully absent. "Affordable Rates" is asserted once as a value prop
  headline, but no pricing tiers, ranges, or examples appear anywhere on
  the public site.
- Gap severity: **High** — this is a direct, unaddressed match to a
  verified pain point, and Explorator's Tier 1/2/3 framework (with
  itemized-estimate framing) maps onto this gap almost exactly.

**4. Service Tiers** (verified brief content)
- Present, but organized by animal type (Small/Companion, Large/Farm,
  Breeding Kennel), not by the wellness → mid → premium tier structure
  Explorator's brief uses. Not wrong, just a different organizing logic —
  worth noting for Strategos rather than treating as a gap.

**5. FAQ/Objections** (verified brief content)
- Site has a Blog section (not reviewed for content in this pass) but no
  visible FAQ page. The triage-checklist angle ("how do I know if my pet
  needs emergency care?") does not appear to exist anywhere on the site.
- Gap severity: **Moderate-High** — a clean opportunity, not currently
  contested by any existing content.

## Highest-Severity Gaps

1. **Opaque Pricing** — zero pricing transparency against a verified,
   named pain point. Most direct match between brief and gap.
2. **Booking friction** — three inconsistent phone numbers, no real
   scheduling system visible publicly.
3. **No triage/emergency-care content** — a clean, currently-unclaimed
   angle that directly matches the door-opener content strategy already
   discussed (the "aha" content approach).

## Open Questions

- Have not reviewed the actual Facebook/Instagram/Twitter content — only
  confirmed the links exist. A full Mode B pass should include a quick
  look at posting frequency and engagement, not just presence.
- Blog content unreviewed — may already partially address FAQ/objection
  gaps; worth checking before assuming the gap is fully open.
- This is public information only. No visibility into actual wait times,
  real pricing, or true booking difficulty — only what's inferable from
  the public-facing presentation.

## Outreach Angle (Mode B specific output)

Given the gaps above, the triage-checklist asset already discussed is a
strong door-opener specifically for Aniworld: it fills a real content gap
(no FAQ/emergency content exists), it's low-risk to hand over free, and it
doesn't touch the two more sensitive gaps (pricing, booking) that would
require a real client relationship to address credibly.
